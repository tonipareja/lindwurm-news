The Lindwurm wolves arrive as a unit built to seize the initiative from the first turn. Their natural place is on the flanks, where they can pressure secondary objectives and force the opponent to split resources before the main line has settled.

On the table, their appeal is the way they combine mobility with sustained threat. They are not just a hard charge. They create an uncomfortable zone around your forward lines and ask the opponent to respect space that looked safe a moment earlier.
